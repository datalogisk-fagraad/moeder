1. Instruktorudfordringen (DIKU-udvalg kommer)
    - After summer, all students have to take two courses in each block, so
      students might not want to be TAs.
    - Sebastian Boring of Communication & IT, Christian Igel and Pawel are
      present as part of the task force.  They have discussed solutions and
      possibilities.
    - Sebastian: Our main problem is recruiting TAs.
    - Hiring students to be TAs have been a little worse than last year.
        1. We think that a student can be a TA and get 7.5 ECTS in one block by
           having a course for TAing.  There's still payment, but you have to do
           a report.
          - Pawel notes that you have to do a little more work than a TA job
            like now.
          - If you become TA for the same course the next year, it'll be easier.
        2. Make PhDs be TAs.  They could spend all their duty hours for TAing.
           Would need 40 PhDs to take all TAs.  This will not be popular.
        3. Block structure doesn't help.  It might help more to spread out over
           a full semester, so the new structure might help.  You might
           alternate the hours in a way, so that you get 135 hours per semester
           instead of per block. (TA half time)
        4. No payment for TAing, ok to only have one course.  A student can only
           have a limited TA jobs, since each job is a 7.5 ECTS course in
           itself.
        5. Blended learning.  Half of the exercises are online.  It allows you
           to work from home. (More official than it is right now.)
        6. Hire external lecturers for TAing.  Might not be well-liked by
           students?  Might do it on evenings.
    - None of these solutions are stand-alone solutions, but instead supplements
      to the existing method which is already used.
    - Rasmus notes that all suggestions are about having the same workload.
      Rasmus suggests that we lessen the workload in a TA job, e.g. by
      automating corrections.
    - Igel notes that the 135 hours are fixed.  NB questions this, as it's
      different on MATH.
    - People disagree whether 135 hours are always the right amount.  Mostly it
      fits, and if it doesn't, you can always speak with your boss.
    - NB notes that you might separate correcting assignments and teaching.
    - Oleks has been a TA on a lot of programming courses.  "I found myself
      copying a lot of feedback.  I think it's underestimated how much feedback
      you can automate.  There's a lot of new technology around that you can
      take a look at."
    - Oleks thinks it might also make the TA job more appealing and interesting
      if the job was also about making tools for autocorrecting assignments.
    - Rasmus notes that Oleks did this for an assignment in Compilers 2014, and
      it worked very well for that task.
    - Rasmus thinks we need to combine the 6 strategies.  Caro also thinks that
      one solution will not work, but that you need a combination.  Sebastian
      notes that this was always the idea, and that the current TA job will
      still work.
    - Oleks notes that the idea of less face time is a good idea, but he still
      gets a lot of face time answers, and that probably won't go away, since
      that's random questions that can't really be automated.  Not having to
      correct exercises would make it easier.
    - Pawel asks if TAs would be happy if they only had to correct assignments.
    - Jenny believes that it would take away some of the integrity to do only
      correcting or teaching, since you wouldn't know them as well.
    - Rasmus agrees that to be able to help the students with the assignments,
      you should know them.
    - Caro notes that it's also difficult to be the one teaching if you don't
      know the state of the corrections, unless you have a strong tie with the
      correcting TA (and that would introduce overhead).
    - Helena thinks it's important to stress that it's very important that the
      TA's are the students, both for the TAs and the attendants at the
      exercises classes.  It can be more comfortable to ask a TA, and TAs might
      have a better idea of the students' level.
    - Helena asks if it's possible to have some kind of a wildcard; a way for
      the TAs to have more favors in taking a course next year if they fail
      because of their TA job.
    - Pawel thinks it will not be possible due to the administration.
    - NB: "What I always thinks takes time when I'm a TA is assignment
      correcting."
    - NB thinks it makes sense to make more space between assignments (not
      weekly).
    - NB suggests that it might make sense to advertise better.  Maybe make an
      event to sell being a TA.
    - Svend: "I assume that it is generally accepted that the new rules are
      good.  Why do they not change those rules?  We cannot have a university
      with rules that are not good."
    - Ken answers that no, the rules are not accepted as the best, but we have
      to make the best with what rules we have right now.
    - Svend suggests that being a TA should allow a student to finish later.
    - Ken says that the department cannot do that.  It's out of our control.
    - Sebastian hears Svend and agrees that we should not accept a university
      with rules that we don't like, but that will not happen in the course of a
      month.
    - Svend: "It's not fun anymore."
    - Pawel doesn't like it either, but doesn't think it's the right time.
      Rasmus suggests that we wait with this point until later.
